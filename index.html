The Habit Loop: Understanding How Habits Form

Why do we automatically do certain things every day?

Habits shape the way we use our phones, study, sleep, exercise, and move through daily life. Once a pattern gets repeated enough, it can start to feel automatic.

What Is a Habit?

A habit is a behavior that becomes automatic through repetition.

The brain forms habits to save effort and make actions easier, so repeated behaviors can start happening with very little thought.

Repeated action → Automatic behavior

The Habit Loop

Cue → Routine → Reward

Cue

A cue starts the loop.

A cue is the trigger that tells your brain to begin a behavior. It can be a sound, feeling, place, time, or situation.
